The NZE (Net Zero Emissions) Goal made by the United Nations in the 2015 Paris Agreement requires countries to work together to enable a worldwide transition away from fossil fuels to renewables for energy.

So which countries are responsible for releasing the most amount of carbon emissions? The top emitter in the world is China, followed by United States and India.

A Snapshot of Energy in Australia

Australia has one of the top ranking carbon emissions per capita, and traditionally has relied on fossil fuels for its energy. In recent years however, there has been an increased speed in the adoption of renewables, with around 11% of energy sourced from solar, wind or other forms of renewable energy in Australia.
